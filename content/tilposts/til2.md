I started today hoping to read the second part of the LLM optimisation blog but decided against that, I dont want to confine myself to only ML and want to actually research on something rather than just reading other's findings in a blog.

One topic that caught my eye was *Webrings*, I came across this topic because seniors in HSP(Homebrew) are building a webring for the HSP and Homebrew sites and a few more sites.
When I first approached this I had no idea what it was and had to search it on google and ended up on the wikipedia page where I understood that it is essentially a cycle or a **ring** that connects a bunch of websites together and basically allows the user to access the next site in the series from the current site and so on, completing a whole ring.
I thought of ouroboros for some reason.

To be part of a webring, all the websites in the ring need to have a common navigation bar that contains the links of the sites, like the previous site and the next site allowing the user to access the other sites in the ring.

> However, the select-through route around the ring is usually supplemented by a central site with links to all member sites; this prevents the ring from breaking completely if a member site goes offline. 

This is very cool according to me cause it shows that the ring is ready for the case where a site may be offline, I believe in the ring being set up by HSP the homebrew site is the central site.

> Webrings are mainly viewed as a relic of the early web of the 1990s.

Well I discovered them today and honestly find them very cool and something I think we should bring back because it not only increases traffic to similar sites but also is a cool way to connect your site to your friends' and also a very cool project that I should look into building.

On further fishing around the wikipedia, I found out that The first "webringo" first appeared in the Internet Archive in September 2006, but failed in September 2011.
This is very cool according to me because I really like the Internet Archive site and their work.

Conclusion - I should try building one with my friends or at the very least try to assist in building the HSP one.
I know that today's TIL wasnt very technical but I enjoyed learning something new and vintage. I will focus on expanding my technical knowledge but I do also enjoy learning small topics like this one.
